PHOTOGRAPHY
- Art form for 2 centuries.
- Art of capturing and processing light using sensors or films.

What is Camera Obscura?
- Latin for "dark room."
- Also known as "Pinhole Image"
- Box shaped device or room that lets light in through a small opening and projects a reversed and inverted image on the other side.

HISTORY OF PHOTOGRAPHY
- 1826- First permanent photograph was captured in 1826 by Joseph Nicephore in France.
- 1829- Joseph Nicephore and Louis Daguerre teamed up to refine photographic methods. 
- 1833- Louis Daguerre created the Daguerreotype, a method that uses silver-plated coppers to produce sharp, direct-positive images.
- 1839- The Daguerreotype became the first commercialized photographic process.

PARTS OF A CAMERA
- Aperture- Opening of the lens
- ISO- determines how sensitive a camera is to light. (e.g. ISO 100, ISO 1600)
- Shutter Speed- Part of camera that opens and close that lets light in to take the picture.
- View Finder- hole in the camera showing the FOV of its lens. It is a vital part of the camera.

TECHNIQUES IN PHOTOGRAPHY
1. Basic Exposure- Controlling how much light reaches your camera sensor or film. Depends on shutter speed and aperture.
2. Shutter Speed- length of time the camera shutter is open while taking a photo.
3. Establishing Focus- When focusing a camera to a subject, it establishes a focus plane. Focus plane refers to imaginary horizontal and vertical lines. Anything within the lines would be in focus and anything outside are not.
4. Position and Angle- Position refers to the height at which your camera is held. Angle refers to how the camera is oriented. 
5. Lighting- Shapes the way images are captured and perceived. There are types of lighting:
	
	a. Natural Light
	b. Artificial Light

6. White Balance- Balances color temperature in image.
7. Rule of Thirds- Leaves your subject to the left or right third of your image, leaving 2/3 of your image open.
8. Picture Style- Setting preset that applies contrast, saturation, sharpening and so on as you shoot an image.
