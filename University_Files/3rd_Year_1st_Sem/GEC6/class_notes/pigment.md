Color
- Light in different wavelengths strike our eyes. Appeals to most critical emotions and moods.

Color Theory
- Art and science of using color.
- Explains how humans perceive color and how colors mix and match with one another.
- Aristotle- Developed theory of colors based on his observations in nature (sunlight, fire, air, & water)

Albert H. Munsell
- Creates the Munsell Color System to describe color (hue, value, chroma).
- Hue- Description of color.
- Value- Lightness/Darkness of a color.
- Chroma- Strength/Saturation of a color.

Color Scheme
- Combination of colors used in a range of design projects.

1. Complementary Colors- Basically opposite colors in a color wheel.

2. Split Complenentary Colors- Complementary color of two colors that are 1 color apart. Combination of colors, not mixture of colors.

3. Analogous Colors- Colors that are adjacent to the color you picked. For example, your color is yellow. Your analogous color is yellow-orange and yellow-green.

4. Triad Colors- 3 Colors that are three colors apart in a color wheel.

Color Psychology
- Study of the psychological effects of colors on human behavior.

1. Red- Life and warmth. Intense.
2. Yellow- Symbolism of gold. Pleasant and cheerful feelings.
3. Green-Stands for awakening, beggining and growth
4. Blue- wide open space and emptiness
5. Purple- Royalty and Priesthood
6. Orange- Vibrant and energetic color. Enthusiasm, creativity, and warmth.
7. Black and White- Death, darkness, power, authority and lucky, purity and cleanliness respectively.
