Sculpture
- artistic form where hard/plastic materials are made into 3D art objects

TECHNIQUES IN SCULPTURE
1. Carving- using tools to shape a material by scraping away potions of it.
2. Subtractive- artist start from a large piece of material and subtracts it until it reaches its desired form.
3. Modeling- Additive process. Artist uses a soft material to build up its shape or form.
4. Additive- Materials are built up to create the sculpture's form.
5. Casting- Using mould and pouring in liquid materials and wait for it to harden to create the sculpture.
6. Constructing and Assembling- Joining preformed pieces of material.
7. Bending
8. Sewing and Stitching

Representational Art- art that represents something.
Non-representational Art- compositions that do not reply to representation of any extent.

TYPES OF SCULPTURE
1.Relief- "relievo". Figure is supported by the background and the figure's base is detached from the ground.
	a. High Relief- Figure protrudes from the background.
	b. Sunken Relief- Figure is sunked in the background.

2. Kinetic Sculpture- Shows movement as a basic element. It utilizes Armature. 
	a. Armature- framework at which a sculpture is built upon. Can be considered as the foundation of a sculpture.

3. Assemblage Sculpture- Found objects are assembled and arranged in a way that creates a piece.
	a. Installation- describe large scale constructions designed for a specific space. Usually occupies a large space.

MATERIALS
1. Stone- earliest form of material dating back to the paleolithic period. Most of the principle types of stones (igneous, sedimentary and metamorphic) are used for sculpting as materials.
2. Jade- specialty of Chinese master craftsmen ever since Neolithic times. Best form of hardstone sculpture.
3. Wood- Oldest and most continuous type of sculpture. Softwood and hardwood are both used for sculptures.
4. Metal- Bronze is usually utilized.
