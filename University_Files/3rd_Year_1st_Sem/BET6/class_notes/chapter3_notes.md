BUSINESS MODELS

* serrve as guiding tools by startup companies to think of better solutions to generate new ideas for their products.

BUSINESS MODEL CANVASS

* proposed by Alexander Osterwalder in his book "Business Model Ontology" which states 9 segments which form the building block for a business.

1. Customer Segments- Who are the customers?
   a. Segment Dimensions: Single/Multi-sided market?
   b. Segment Composition: Personas (characterizing individuals through their personalities)
   c. Problem, Needs, Habit and Current Alternatives: What need are you fulfilling for the customers.
2. Value Proposition- value the company promises to give to their customers.
3. Channels- how the company communicates with their customers to deliver their value proposition.

   CHANNEL PHASES:
   a. Awareness- advertising through word of mouth, social medias and general ads.
   b. Evaluation- conduct an evaluation to assess the company's value proposition.
   c. Purchase- various options for customers to purchase company's products.
   d. Delivery- convenience and faster deliver of orders
   e. After Sales- Post-purchase Customer support.

4. Customer Relationships- important aspect in doing business because your customer will be able to interact with you through questions about the product/service.

   BENEFITS OF GOOD CUSTOMER RELATIONSHIP
   a. Customer Retention
   b. Customer Loyalty- Good customer service = repeat customers that are nine times more likely.
   c. Customer Satisfaction- Open channel of communication for relaying customer feedback.

5. Revenue Streams- cash the compnayt generates from each customer segment.

   HOW TO GENERATE REVENUE STREAMS

   1. Sale of Physical Product- Customer pays in cash for the product.
   2. Usage Fee- customer pays a user fee for a particular service.
   3. Subscription fee- cusomter pays a fee for a service for a specific time frame only.
   4. Lending/renting/leasing- rights to use a product for a fixed period of time
   5. Brokerage fees- company gets revenue from an intermediate service.
   6. Advertising- business may charge fees for advertising a product or service from different company.
   7. Volume and Unit selling- same as sale of physical product but can be cheaper if sold at volume.

6. Key Activities- Most important tasks a company must carry out.
7. Key Resources- main inputs that a company utilizes to create its value proposition and sell their service or product to the customers.

   TYPES OF KEY RESOURCES
   a. Physical Resources- tangible resources. Buildings, equipment, plants and so on.
   b. Intellectual Resources- Non-physical sources like brand patents, copyrights and partnerships.
   c. Human Resources- people behind the business.
   d. Financial Resources.

8. Key Partnerships- relationships with other businesses, and organizational entities that help your business model work.

   TYPES OF PARTNERSHIPS
   a. Strategic Alliances Between Non-Competitors- two companies with harmonious relationship.
   b. Coopetition- Combination of competition and cooperation.
   c. Joint Venture to Develop New Businesses- Company may join another company to create  a different entity.
   d. Buyer-Supplier Relationship- Trust and commitment between buyer and supplier.

9. Cost Structure- Defines all costs and expenses that your company will eat to operate your business.

   CATEGORIES OF COST STRUCTURE
   a. Value-Driven- Create more value in the product itself.
   b. Cost-Driven- Focus on minimizing costs as much as possible.
